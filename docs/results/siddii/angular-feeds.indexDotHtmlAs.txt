             <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Lea_Houston" title="Margaret Lea Houston">\n                Margaret Lea Houston\n               </a>\n              </b>\n              (April\xc2\xa011,            </div>\n              </div>\n              <p>\n               <b>\n                <a href="/wiki/Noel_Park" title="Noel Park">\n                 Noel Park\n                </a>\n               </b>\n               in north London is a\n               <a href="/wiki/Planned_community" title="Planned community">\n                planned community\n               </a>\n               designed by\n               <a href="/wiki/Rowland_Plumbe" title="Rowland Plumbe">\n                Rowland Plumbe\n               </a>\n               in the late 19th century, consisting of 2,200\n               <a href="/wiki/Model_dwellings_company" title="Model dwellings company">\n                model dwellings\n               </a>\n               . It was developed in open countryside to the north of London between the historic villages of\n               <a href="/wiki/Highgate" title="Highgate">\n                Highgate\n               </a>\n               and\n               <a href="/wiki/Tottenham" title="Tottenham">\n                Tottenham\n               </a>\n               by the\n               <a href="/wiki/Artizans,_Labourers_%26_General_Dwellings_Company" title="Artizans, Labourers &amp; General Dwellings Company">\n                Artizans, Labourers &amp; General Dwellings Company\n               </a>\n               . One of the earliest\n               <a href="/wiki/Garden_city_movement" title="Garden city movement">\n                garden suburbs\n               </a>\n               in the world, it provided affordable housing for working-class families wishing to leave the inner city. Every property had a front and rear garden, and it was  close enough to the rail network to allow its residents to commute to work. In line with the principles of the company\'s founder, William Austin, it had no\n               <a href="/wiki/Pub" title="Pub">\n                pubs\n               </a>\n               , and there are still none today. As a result of London\'s rapid expansion during the early 20th century, and particularly after the\n               <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">\n                London Underground\n               </a>\n               was extended to the area in 1932, Noel Park was surrounded by later developments. In 1965, it was incorporated into the newly created\n               <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Haringey" title="London Borough of Haringey">\n                London Borough of Haringey\n               </a>\n               . Despite damage during\n               <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n                World War II\n               </a>\n               and demolition work during the construction of\n               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Mall_(Wood_Green)" title="The Mall (Wood Green)">\n                Wood Green Shopping City\n               </a>\n               in the 1970s,              <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Lea_Houston" title="Margaret Lea Houston">\n                Margaret Lea Houston\n               </a>\n              </b>\n              (April\xc2\xa011, 1819\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 December\xc2\xa03, 1867) was First Lady of the\n              <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Texas" title="Republic of Texas">\n               Republic of Texas\n              </a>\n              , First Lady of the state of\n              <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n               Texas\n              </a>\n              , and a founding member of Concord Baptist Church in Grand Cane. She was a poet and an accomplished musician. Her influence on husband\n              <a href="/wiki/Sam_Houston" title="Sam Houston">\n               Sam Houston\n              </a>\n              persuaded him to give up alcohol and profane language. Margaret gave birth inside the governor\'s mansion to the youngest of their eight children, as angry mobs gathered outside in response to her husband\'s opposition to Texas signing the\n              <a href="/wiki/Ordinance_of_Secession" title="Ordinance of Secession">\n               Ordinance of Secession\n              </a>\n              of the\n              <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_War_in_the_United_States" title="Civil War in the United States">\n               Civil War\n              </a>\n              . He was removed from office for refusing to swear loyalty to the Confederacy. Their eldest son joined the Confederate army and was left for dead on the battlefield at\n              <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shiloh" title="Battle of Shiloh">\n               Shiloh\n              </a>\n              , saved by a Union Army clergyman who found a Bible from Margaret in his pocket. Sam Houston died of illness before the end of the war, as Margaret sat by his bed reading to him from the\n              <a href="/wiki/Psalm_23" title="Psalm 23">\n               23rd Psalm\n              </a>\n              . She became the keeper of his legacy and opened his records to a trusted biographer. When she died of\n              <a href="/wiki/Yellow_fever" title="Yellow fever">\n               yellow fever\n              </a>\n              , Margaret could not be buried with her husband in a public cemetery for fear of contamination, and was instead interred on private  family property. 2x" width="150"/>\n                </a>\n               </div>\n              </div>\n              <p>\n               <b>\n                <a href="/wiki/Everglades_National_Park" title="Everglades National Park">\n                 Everglades National Park\n                </a>\n               </b>\n               is a\n               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._National_Park" title="U.S. National Park">\n                U.S. National Park\n               </a>\n               in\n               <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n                Florida\n               </a>\n               that protects the southern 20\xc2\xa0percent of the original\n               <a href="/wiki/Everglades" title="Everglades">\n                Everglades\n               </a>\n               . It is the largest tropical wilderness in the United States and the third-largest national park in the lower 48\xc2\xa0states after\n               <a href="/wiki/Death_Valley_National_Park" title="Death Valley National Park">\n                Death Valley\n               </a>\n               and\n               <a href="/wiki/Yellowstone_National_Park" title="Yellowstone National Park">\n                Yellowstone\n               </a>\n               . The park was established in 1934 when canal-building projects in South Florida were threatening the quickly vanishing Everglades, a network of wetlands and forests fed by a river flowing out of\n               <a href="/wiki/Lake_Okeechobee" title="Lake Okeechobee">\n                Lake Okeechobee\n               </a>\n               into\n               <a href="/wiki/Florida_Bay" title="Florida Bay">\n                Florida Bay\n               </a>\n               . It was the first national park created to protect a fragile\n               <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">\n                ecosystem\n               </a>\n               . The park is the most significant breeding ground for tropical\n               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wading_bird" title="Wading bird">\n                wading birds\n               </a>\n               in North America and contains the largest\n               <a href="/wiki/Florida_mangroves" title="Florida mangroves">\n                mangrove\n               </a>\n               ecosystem in the western hemisphere. It is home to 36\n               <a href="/wiki/Threatened_species" title="Threatened species">\n                threatened\n               </a>\n               or protected species including the\n               <a href="/wiki/Florida_panther" title="Florida panther">\n                Florida panther\n               </a>\n               , the\n               <a href="/wiki/American_crocodile" title="American crocodile">\n                American crocodile\n               </a>\n               , and the\n               <a href="/wiki/West_Indian_manatee" title="West Indian manatee">\n                West Indian manatee\n               </a>\n               , and supports 350\xc2\xa0species of birds, 300\xc2\xa0species of fresh and saltwater fish, 40\xc2\xa0species of mammals, and 50\xc2\xa0species of reptiles. Most of South Florida\'s fresh water comes from the\n               <a href="/wiki/Biscayne_Aquifer" title="Biscayne Aquifer">\n                Biscayne Aquifer\n               </a>\n               , which is replenished by the Everglades.            <b>\n                <a href="/wiki/Donnchadh,_Earl_of_Carrick" title="Donnchadh, Earl of Carrick">\n                 Donnchadh\n                </a>\n               </b>\n class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gall-Gaidhil" title="Gall-Gaidhil">\n                Gall-Gaidhil\n               </a>\n               prince and one of the longest serving\n               <a His father,\n               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gille-Brighde_of_Galloway" title="Gille-Brighde of Galloway">\n                Gille-Brighde of Galloway\n               </a>\n               , and his uncle,\n               <a href="/wiki/Uhtred_of_Galloway" title="Uhtred of Galloway">\n                Uhtred of Galloway\n               </a>\n               , were the two rival sons of\n               <a href="/wiki/Fergus_of_Galloway" title="Fergus of Galloway">\n                Fergus\n               </a>\n               , Lord of\n               <a href="/wiki/Galloway" title="Galloway">\n                Galloway\n               </a>\n               . Although denied succession over all the lands of Galloway, he was granted lordship over\n               <a href="/wiki/Carrick,_Scotland" title="Carrick, Scotland">\n                Carrick\n               </a>\n               in the north. A patron of religious houses, particularly\n               <a href="/wiki/Melrose_Abbey" title="Melrose Abbey">\n                Melrose Abbey\n               </a>\n               and\n               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Berwick_priory" title="North Berwick priory">\n                North Berwick priory\n               </a>\n               <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nunnery" title="Nunnery">\n                nunnery\n               </a>\n               , he attempted to establish a\n               <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">\n                monastery\n               </a>\n               in his own territory, at\n               <a href="/wiki/Crossraguel_Abbey" title="Crossraguel Abbey">\n                Crossraguel\n               </a>\n               .
